This document defines the player character (pilot), their attributes, progression, and role in the game.

## Pilot Attributes

### Identity
- Name (player-chosen)
- Background / Origin story
- Portrait or appearance (if applicable)

### Core Stats
- **Credits** — Currency for trading, purchases, repairs
- **Reputation** — Standing with factions and regions; affects NPC interactions, prices, and access
- **Experience / Level** — Overall progression tracker

### Skills
- **Trading** — Better prices, market insight, price trend visibility
- **Piloting** — Maneuverability in combat, evasion chance
- **Engineering** — Cheaper repairs, ability to self-repair, component maintenance
- **Negotiation** — Better mission rewards, NPC interactions, rescue outcomes
- **Combat** — Weapon accuracy, damage output, combat encounter success
- **Exploration** — Improved search outcomes, scanner effectiveness, discovery quality

## Pilot Progression

### Experience System
- XP earned from: trading profits, completing missions, discovering locations, winning combat, clearing hostile systems
- Leveling up grants skill points for manual allocation
- Clearing a hostile system provides significant XP (rewards progression)

### Reputation System
- Reputation tracked per **faction and region**
- Reputation gained by: completing missions, trading, rescuing NPCs, clearing hostile systems
- Reputation lost by: smuggling contraband, attacking friendlies, failing missions
- Reputation affects:
  - Access to exclusive missions at hub systems
  - Better prices at allied merchants
  - Hostile encounters with enemy factions
  - NPC dialogue and information sharing
  - Access to restricted system areas

## Pilot Backgrounds

### Starting Background Options
All pilots begin the game with the same **tutorial scenario** — stranded with a broken ship in a sub-system. However, background choice affects starting bonuses and skill inclinations.

| Background | Starting Bonus | Skill Boost | Lore |
|------------|---------------|-------------|------|
| Merchant | Extra credits | Trading | Former corporate trader making deliveries |
| Explorer | Scanner upgrade | Exploration | Charted unknown systems for a living |
| Smuggler | Stealth mod | Negotiation | Ran contraband through hostile territory |
| Mechanic | Repair kit | Engineering | Built and repaired ships at a shipyard |
| Military | Weapon upgrade | Combat | Discharged veteran of a faction war |

All backgrounds share the same tutorial: the delivery run that went wrong.

## Death and Respawn

### What Happens When the Pilot Dies
1. Player respawns at the **last visited merchant**
2. Receives a **basic loaner/escape pod** ship
3. Original ship and remaining cargo (minus 0-50% looted) stay at death location
4. Player can buy a new ship at the hub if they have credits
5. No permanent stat or skill loss — death is a setback, not a reset
6. Player is always able to recover

## Pilot Log / Journal

### Tracked Information
- All visited systems (with 6-digit addresses)
- Discovery progress per system (X / Y locations)
- Completed missions and quest history
- Known system addresses (from bulletin boards, NPCs, star charts)
- Trade price history and trends
- Statistics: total profit, systems visited, discoveries made, enemies defeated, hostile systems cleared

### Journal as Gameplay Tool
- The log serves as the player's **personal star chart**
- Previously discovered addresses are always accessible
- Price history helps inform trading decisions
- Discovery progress motivates returning to partially explored systems
